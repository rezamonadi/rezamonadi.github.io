Reza Monadi

I am a full-time lecturer here at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo. I was born in Rasht, a culturally rich city less than 30 km (20 mi) south of the Caspian Sea coast. I fell in love with astronomy as a child when my parents enrolled me in an astronomy summer school. Living in a mostly cloudy city, my love for astronomy led me to pursue radio astronomy. I studied radio pulsars for my master's thesis. After teaching gifted high schoolers for a couple of years, Asieh (my wife and best friend) and I moved to Riverside, California, where I received a scholarship to pursue my PhD research on quasars and the intergalactic medium. I love biking, especially downhill mountain biking!

Projects

Extremely Red Quasars: We used unsupervised machine learning methods to cluster this group of quasars in parameter space and study the properties of their spectra.

Metal Lines Catalog: With the help of state-of-the-art machine learning techniques such as Gaussian processes, we are trying to create useful catalogs using spectroscopic data available in datasets like SDSS.
